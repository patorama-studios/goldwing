The Opportunity

You're already ahead. The competition just hasn't noticed yet.

Independent research across the Australian motorcycle club landscape uncovered something unexpected — and very, very good news for the Association.

Australian Clubs Offer Feature-Gated Paid Tiers.

Ulysses Club runs a single tier. BMW Motorcycle Club of Victoria runs three eligibility tiers — not feature tiers. Triumph Owners Motorcycle Club Australia runs two. Oz HOG mirrors its global parent.

None has built a true premium experience for members willing to pay more for more. That is your opening.

The Strategy

Three Pillars. One Destination.

Goldwing's existing platform already exceeds every comparable Australian club on portal sophistication. The growth opportunity isn't more features — it's three specific gaps where international peers like GWOCGB (UK) and BMWMCV's training programs are clearly ahead.

Pillar 01

Commercial Monetisation

Build an 8–11 partner sponsor roster across insurance, parts, trailers, touring gear and Honda Australia — directly modelled on the UK Gold Wing Owners Club's 11-partner program (Bike Sure, Carole Nash, Howden, Honda UK, David Silver Spares).

Layered with member-discount codes, lead-gen forms and tiered sponsor placements — the website stops being a cost and starts being a revenue engine.

Modelled on GWOCGB · UK
Pillar 02

Signature Content

A hand-curated library of Australian Goldwing-friendly touring routes — sealed roads, smooth twisties, motel parking, fuel and coffee stops — built into the portal with planner-to-app sync.

REVER built a 3,000-route global library that became their signature asset. No Australian motorcycle club owns the equivalent. The Association can.

Modelled on REVER × Butler Maps
Pillar 03

Training & Safety Benefits

A rider-training subsidy program ($50–$80 every three years), a marque-aligned touring-skills training partnership, and a club-owned satellite phone for members heading into remote country.

Ulysses, BMWMCV and BMW Canberra all run versions of these. They are universal table-stakes that the Association currently lacks — and they cost almost nothing per member to deliver.

Modelled on Ulysses · BMWMCV · Touratech
Where You Are Today

A Foundation Most Associations Envy.

Patorama Studios has spent the past 18 months building the Australian Goldwing Association a bespoke member platform that the competitive research found already exceeds every comparable Australian club. The work below isn't a list of features — it's a structural advantage.

  • Member portal with secure login and personalised dashboard
  • My Garage — bike tracking and rider profile
  • Events calendar with ride RSVP and admin management
  • Membership checkout via Stripe (cards, recurring, refunds)
  • Admin dashboard for committee operations
  • AGM page builder for the annual meeting
  • Blog and content management system
  • Wing magazine integration
  • Wings preference filter and member-portal lockdown
  • Audit-grade activity logging and member directory

The Research Finding That Matters Most.

Ulysses Club's digital offering is materially thinner than its reputation. The Triumph Owners Motorcycle Club Australia has no member portal at all — just a PDF membership form and Facebook. BMW Motorcycle Club of Victoria runs on off-the-shelf WildApricot SaaS.

The Association's bespoke stack — garage tracking, Stripe checkout, AGM builder, custom CMS — is already a category-leading foundation among Australian marque memberships.

"On portal features the Australian Goldwing Association is already ahead of every comparable Australian club surveyed. The opportunity is not to build more — it is to monetise and differentiate what already exists."
